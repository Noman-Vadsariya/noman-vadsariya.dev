## Problem

Long-form video is hard to index, search, and make accessible. Existing captioning models tend to produce either single global captions or dense captions without meaningful temporal grounding.

## Approach

- OpenCV for frame extraction and temporal segmentation.
- A transformer-based seq2seq model in PyTorch, trained end-to-end to emit timestamp-anchored caption sequences.
- Temporal attention layer to focus on relevant segments rather than averaging across the whole clip.
- Training pipeline handled preprocessing, alignment, and evaluation across a large video dataset.

## Impact

- BLEU score up **12%** vs. the baseline captioning model.
- Timestamp-anchored output made the captions usable for automated indexing, summarization, and accessibility use cases.

## What I learned

- Temporal modeling is fundamentally different from text-only seq2seq — alignment and segmentation choices dominate quality.
- End-to-end pipelines for video demand careful thought about I/O bottlenecks; the model wasn't the slowest part.
- Robust evaluation matters more than most single metric improvements suggest — BLEU alone is misleading for grounded captions.
