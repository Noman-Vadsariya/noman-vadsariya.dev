## Problem

Off-the-shelf toxicity classifiers (including ToxicBERT and BERT fine-tunes) fire disproportionately often on African-American English (AAE), inflating false positives for a specific dialect group. The goal was to shrink that gap on **TwitterAAE** and **HateXplain** without giving up overall accuracy.

## Approach

Built baselines and a spectrum of fairness-aware interventions on top of BERT embeddings:

- **Baselines** — XGBoost on text embeddings, fine-tuned BERT, and pretrained ToxicBERT for reference.
- **Reweighting** — up-weight AAE non-toxic examples to push down false positives on that group.
- **Fairness-aware training** — joint loss combining BCE for accuracy with a **group-gap penalty** for AAE/SAE disparity, gated by a γ trade-off.
- **Group-conditional thresholds** — different decision cut-offs per dialect at inference time.
- **Vector scaling (VS)** on XGBoost, plus a **ternary "toxic / offensive / not-toxic"** setup with manual and auto class weights.
- **Uncertainty band ("unsure")** — abstain around the decision boundary at 0.45–0.55, 0.40–0.60, 0.35–0.65.
- **Adversarial debiasing** — Equalized-Odds projection with both a linear adversary on XGBoost and an MLP adversary on top of BERT embeddings, tuned over a Slurm grid search.

## Evaluation

- In-domain: balanced and unbalanced TwitterAAE splits.
- Out-of-domain: trained on TwitterAAE, evaluated on **HateXplain** for generalization under covariate shift.
- Metrics: F1, selective-F1, coverage, EO gap (FPR/FNR disparity), abstain-aware gap for the three-class variant.

## What I took away

- Reweighting alone shifts the AAE FPR but tends to trade it for lower overall performance — you have to combine it with calibration or an adversary to hold the accuracy line.
- Adversarial debiasing with EO projection was the most robust: it kept working under distribution shift (TwitterAAE → HateXplain), which the simpler methods did not.
- Abstain / "unsure" bands are underrated. Giving the model permission to defer on borderline cases cut disparate impact substantially at a modest coverage cost.
- Fairness experiments live and die on infrastructure — the Slurm grid + aggregation scripts were as important as the model choices for getting reproducible results.
