## Question

Given a corpus of documents at inference time, is it better to:

1. **Encode the documents into the model** via a **Doc2LoRA hypernetwork** that emits per-document LoRA adapters, or
2. **Keep documents external** and rely on **in-context RAG** (retrieve → rerank → generate)?

We benchmarked both across three regimes: **HotpotQA** (multi-hop QA), **ASQA** (long-form ambiguous QA), and a **synthetic needle-in-a-haystack** stress test.

## Approach

- **Data pipeline** — HuggingFace-sourced HotpotQA and ASQA, plus custom NIAH; teacher models **Qwen** and **Gemma** generate additional QA pairs used to train the hypernetwork.
- **Hypernetwork training** — two-stage:
  - *Stage 1* adapts the Doc2LoRA base to the target dataset.
  - *Stage 2* applies **chunked-context fine-tuning** so the hypernetwork learns to compress long documents into LoRA weights.
- **RAG baselines** — four configurations:
  - End-to-end retrieval + generation.
  - Generation-only with cached retrieval (to isolate reader quality).
  - Gold-context (upper bound).
  - Needle-in-a-haystack (retrieval stress test).
- **Retrieval stack** — **ColBERT** reranker on top of a **neural SPLADE** dense/sparse retriever, evaluated at multiple top-k values.
- **Infra** — 4× RTX A6000 for hypernetwork training and evaluation, P100s for dev; all training driven by Slurm job submission with cached retrievals in `data/retrieved/`.

## Metrics

Exact Match, **F1**, **ROUGE-L**, and answer containment. Final headline numbers focus on F1 and ROUGE-L.

## What I took away

- **Retrieval quality dominates** end-to-end scores in the in-context RAG setting — swapping SPLADE→ColBERT rerankers moved metrics more than most reader-side changes.
- **Hypernetwork adapters excel when the corpus is stable** and inference latency matters — you pay training cost once, then avoid retrieval at inference.
- **Needle-in-a-haystack** cleanly separated the two paradigms: retrieval-based RAG degrades sharply as noise grows; hypernetwork adapters degrade more gracefully but bottleneck on training-time coverage.
- The gap between **gold-context** and **retrieval-based** F1 is the honest upper bound on how much better a smarter retriever can make you — a useful north star for RAG projects.
- Reproducibility for GPU-heavy comparisons requires disciplined Slurm scripts and cached artifacts; without them, a fair A/B is basically impossible.
